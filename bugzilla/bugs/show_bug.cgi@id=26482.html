Screenshot of unwanted colour blocks

I customised the icons of some folders in Thunderbird (62.6.0 32-bit, on
Windows 10 64-bit) to make them stand out. The icons were a solid square of
colour 64 x 64, .ico.
These were displayed in the quickfolders tabs as very long and very intrusive
bars of colour.
The icons are therefore not represented correctly, and looking through the
options there seemed to be no way to turn them off. Representing them correctly
would improve things, but turning them off is still preferable: the consume
space and were used to make critical folders stand out in the thunderbird tree.
I attach a screenshot of the symptoms.
Cheers,
David

Yes it is a little tricky to find out the actual sizes; I am assuming if
somebody is brave enough to change the way Thunderbird looks they can probably
have some efreedom (I can potentially imagine using 20px or 24px icons if you
have large fonts) so I do not really want to "force-resize" all icons to 16px.
This way gives more freedom to the users.

> The other thing, is the ability to turn icons off, which I couldn't spot. 

You can remove the "customized" Icons, onee by onee, but the special ones (for
folders such as Inbox / Sent / Junk etc) can be eturned off on the third
"theming" tab of the QuickFolders options, titled "Bling My Tabs!". There is a
setting

 [x] Display Icons

which is meant to hide those default icons.
