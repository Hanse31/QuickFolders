Description:   Opened: 2012-11-17 09:42 
Hi Axel:

A while ago I had requested a feature on Quickfilters and you responded that
the feature would be better suited to Quickfolders.

The feature I had requested was a mail alert that would remain "pending" until
it was acknowledged.  I think the ability to route incoming mail to specific
folders is a great idea however, on certain folders it is almost imperative
that a notice of the arrival of such mail stays visible until it is
acknowledge.  I have an alert add on now but if I'm not at my PC when mail
arrives I obviously miss seeing i.

Now, I really hope that I don't embarrass myself if you write back and inform
me that it is in there already.   I always apply the upgrades but sometimes do
not get to read all of the changes with each release.  If it's not there yet I
really hope it makes it to your "to do" list.

Your add=ons are great, thank you for your efforts.

Regards,

Frank
------- Comment #1 From Axel Grude 2012-11-17 13:40:21 ------- 
I have renamed the title slightly, I think what you mean is that (over an above
showing that a folder has unread mail), you want a persistant flag "more unread
mail has arrived" that should only be reset

a) manually (e.g. by a click on a context menu command such as "clear new mail
alert"
b) by reading all email in the folder or using the "mark all mail as read"
command

This is something that could be stored in the quickFolder tab itself as a
special property. As a first working draft I would suggest that it should be
shown by a special "flag" icon, I will attach a screenshot later.

I think this is a valuable suggestion, the difficult part here is to design a
simple User Interface in a way that the ordinary user understands what this
feature is about.

That's one possible way.  I was thinking about slightly differently.  Rather
than have alerts on a folder basis (all emails going to a specific folder fall
into this category) that somehow in conjunction with quickfilters that incoming
emails could be scanned on whatever criteria the user desired and an "Alert"
flag be assigned to be handled by Quickfolders.  

A special Qfolders Alerts tab could be used to keep track (with links) to
whatever email(s) had the Alert flag assigned.  Clicking on the link could open
the email and reset the flag.  One would only have to look at the Alerts tab to
see if there were new emails with alerts and go to the tab and click on the
desired link.  The email would be in the target folder and not have to be moved
where ever one wants it stored.  The reasoning here is that all emails that are
stored in a specific folder do not need alerts and by leaving the alert
mechanism independent of where the email gets stored is the best of both
worlds.

I fully realize that it's easy to think of lots of ways of doing things when
one doesn't need to be concerned about implementation details.

So what you are saying is that you want the filter action to determine "what"
gets flagged, and you're not flagging a whole folder, but the individual email,
based on a filter? In that case, you can already do this - you do not need any
extension for it; in your filter, under "Perform these actions", select "Tag
Message" and then select "Important" in the drop down beside it. (You can
create your own flags, so you can also name it "Alert" or whatever) 

> A special Qfolders Alerts tab could be used to keep track (with links) to
> whatever email(s) had the Alert flag assigned.  

Again, this is already possible, based on your "tagged" emails from above you
can simply make a global search based on that "Important" Tag and save it as
saved search folder. Move the folder to the QuickFolders bar, and voila!
Obviously this is a (search) folder full of emails, and not an individual link
to ONE email. QuickFolders currently does not support linking "single emails"
to it, and I am not sure if it is ever going to support it.

> Clicking on the link could open the email and reset the flag.  
> One would only have to look at the Alerts tab to
> see if there were new emails with alerts and go to the tab and click on the
> desired link.

So this is a little more tricky, basically what you want is to remove the
"Important" Tag automagically as soon as the email is viewed (when it looses
its readonly status). Not sure whether it is possible but there might be also a
way to do this with a filter... you should also download FiltaQuilla and look
at the additional options that this gives you.
