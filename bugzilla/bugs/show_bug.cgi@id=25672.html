Description:   Opened: 2014-01-02 18:00 
In QuickFolders Settings->Bling My Tabs! tab, the bottom section allows you
change the color settings for your QuickFolder tabs.  In addition to settings
for tabs you've created, there are three special color settings, for the Active
tab, for when the mouse hovers over a tab, and for when you drag another tab
over a tab.

For each of these, you can choose one of four color palette settings:

1. Like inactive tab, in which case the color will be whatever color the
inactive tab is set to.
2. 2 Colors, where you choose the background and foreground colors.
3. Standard Palette, where you can choose from the predefined standard color
palette.
4. Pastel Colors, where you choose from the predefined pastel color palette.

Choices 1 and 4 are broken.  Whatever your color settings are for inactive tabs
or whichever color you choose from the Pastel color palette, it doesn't work.

To recreate this bug, using Active Tab Color as an example:

1. Open QuickFolder settings and go the the Bling My Tabs! tab.
2. Go to "Active Tab Color" and select Standard Palette from the pulldown menu.
3. Click on "Active Tab Color" and select Color 13, "rust".
4. Go to your QuickFolders bar and click on Inbox.  The Inbox tab will turn the
rust red defined in the Standard Palette.
5. Go back to the same setting and choose Pastel Colors from the pulldown menu.
6. Click on "Active Tab Color" again and choose Color 6, "moss".
7. Go to your mail folders list, and select any folder other than the Inbox.
8. Go back to the QuickFolders bar and click on Inbox again.

The Inbox tab will turn RUST RED, NOT moss green.  Whatever color you last
chose from the Standard Palette, it will remain in effect.  The active tab
won't use the pastel color you chose.

Similarly, if you chose to use the inactive tab color, instead of the pastel
colors, it will STILL remain rust red.

Finally, the 2 Colors setting does work.  If you choose the 2 Colors setting
and then switch to Pastel or Like Inactive Tab, the 2 color setting will remain
in effect, just like the Standard Palette setting.
