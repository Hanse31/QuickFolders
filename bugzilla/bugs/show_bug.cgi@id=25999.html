Description:   Opened: 2015-04-01 01:59 
For a few weeks I have been losing many emails when trying to transfer them
from my inbox to another folder either by dragging to QuickFolders or dragging
to the Thunderbird folder directly. I thought it was a QF problem, but
disabling QF did not get rid of the problem. In the end I disabled all my
add-ons and Thunderbird started behaving properly. One by one I reinstalled the
3 add-ons I use most, including QF and everything worked OK. I have concluded
the the problem add-on is CompactHeader and I think you need to know about this
behaviour.
------- Comment #1 From Axel Grude 2015-04-01 02:10:38 ------- 
(In reply to comment #0)
> For a few weeks I have been losing many emails when trying to transfer them
> from my inbox to another folder either by dragging to QuickFolders or dragging
> to the Thunderbird folder directly.

I strongly advise to use the "folder repair" feature - emails do not just
"vanish" it is usually the index / gloda that became corrupted. Also QF does
not fundamentally change the way that THunderbird copies / moves stuff; it just
adds a nice UI experience. [QF Tabs are not "folders" they just are attractive
looking shortcuts to the actual folders in Tb]

The other thing you might check out is whether you have sync issues - are you
using IMAP or POP3? I generally prefer POP3 due to a slow connection; I found
it a lot more reliable than IMAP.


> I thought it was a QF problem, but

Unusual, I have used CompactHeader for many years now. As far as I am aware it
only changes visuals (overloads the header box to make it look different). It
also shouldn't interfere with the underlying mechanisms on mail.

As it isn't a QF bug, I am changing status to "WORKSFORME" if that is okay with
you.
